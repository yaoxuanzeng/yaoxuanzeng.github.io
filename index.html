I am currently a graduate student in the Department of the Geophysical Sciences at the University of Chicago.

I study fluid dynamics on different planets, including ocean circulation on icy moons, jets on giant planets, and ocean dynamics on tidally-locked exoplanets.

Before coming to Chicago, I received my B.S. in Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences from Peking University in 2020. I was also a GFD Fellow at Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution in summer 2023.

I will join Wanying Kang's group in September, 2026, studying dynamics and tracer transport in icy moon oceans.

Research

My work sits at the intersection of planetary science, geophysical fluid dynamics, and physical oceanography. Recent topics include slantwise convection, baroclinic eddies, ocean circulation on icy moons, and large-scale flows on giant planets.
